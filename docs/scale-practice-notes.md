## 1. The reach principle (why most of the old shifts were nonsense)

A 2-octave scale on the violin runs from a low string up to the E string. Whether it
fits **within 1stвҖ“3rd position** depends on one thing: **is the top note reachable?**

Highest note reachable per position (4th finger on the E string, no stretch):

| Position | E-string fingers          | Top note |
|----------|---------------------------|----------|
| 1st      | 0=E5 1=FвҷҜ5 2=G5 3=A5 4=B5  | **B5**   |
| 2nd      | 1=GвҷҜ5 2=A5 3=B5 4=C6       | **C6**   |
| 3rd      | 1=A5 2=B5 3=C6 4=D6        | **D6**   |

So, going up only to 3rd position, the **highest playable note is D6**.

A 2-octave scale is therefore:

- **Playable in 1st position alone** if its top note is **вүӨ B5** вҶ’ G, A, Bвҷӯ, B, Aвҷӯ major;
  G, B minor. (No shift required вҖ” any shift here is *optional* practice.)
- **Playable but needs a shift to 3rd position** if its top is **C6 or D6** вҶ’ C, D major;
  C, D minor.
- **Not playable in full 2 octaves below 4th position** if its top is **above D6** вҶ’
  Eвҷӯ, E, F major; E, FвҷҜ minor. Don't stop at one octave вҖ” play the **full first octave,
  then shift to 3rd position and continue up the 2nd octave as far as the hand reaches
  (to D6, or CвҷҜ6 for E major), then turn around and descend.** Only the top one or two
  notes of the scale are dropped, and you still train the shift.

This is exactly your observation: **D is the highest full 2-octave scale; E and F are not
possible in 3rd position.**

## 2. Position / finger reference

Open strings: **G3  D4  A4  E5**.
`(low)` = finger pulled back a half-step (flats / lowered notes).
`(high)` = finger reached forward a half-step (sharps / raised notes).

### 1st position (natural notes)

| Finger | G string | D string | A string | E string |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|----------|

## 3. How to read the scale blocks

Each note line is: `note   string   finger`. Strings are G / D / A / E.
A `в”Җв”Җ shift to Nth position в”Җв”Җ` line marks a position change. Descend = reverse the list
(shift back down at the same point). Open strings shown as `0 (open)`.

## 5. Level 3 scales (1st вҶ’ 2nd вҶ’ 3rd position)

**The ceiling is still D6.** Adding 2nd position does **not** extend range вҖ” it only adds a
middle waypoint for shifting and alternative fingerings. So the 2-octave / 1-octave
verdicts from Level 2 are unchanged. 2nd position's real job here is *technique*: practice
each scale through different positions and practice the 1stвҶ’2nd and 2ndвҶ’3rd shifts.

### Using 2nd position вҖ” example: one-octave G major entirely in 2nd position
```
G4   D   2   (2nd pos)
A4   D   3   (2nd pos)
B4   D   4   (2nd pos)
C5   A   1 (low, 2nd pos)
D5   A   2   (2nd pos)
E5   A   3   (2nd pos)
FвҷҜ5  A   4   (2nd pos)
G5   E   1 (low, 2nd pos)
```
A good drill: play a one-octave scale you already know entirely in 1st position, then
entirely in 2nd, then 3rd, checking each note against an open string. The
"1stвҶ’2ndвҶ’3rd in one scale" idea from the old doc is best treated as *graduated shift
drills*, not a single mandatory fingering вҖ” forcing all three positions into one scale
produces contrived fingerings no one uses.

### Scales reused from Level 2
G, D, A, Bвҷӯ major and B, C, D minor use the **same note-and-finger layouts as Level 2
above** (same notes, same reach verdicts). At Level 3, add the position drills.

## 6. Corrections vs. `scale-practice-method.md`

The old Level 2/3 tables assigned one "shift point" per scale as if canonical. Most were
wrong in one of two ways:

1. **Fake shifts on scales that need none.** G, A, Bвҷӯ, B (and G, B minor) fit entirely in
   1st position. Their listed shifts ("shift on B / CвҷҜ / D вҖҰ") are optional practice at
   best, not required.
2. **Impossible 2-octave claims.** E major, F major, Eвҷӯ major, E minor, FвҷҜ minor top out
   above D6 and cannot be completed in a *full* 2 octaves below 4th position. The old doc
   gave them 2 octaves and invented shift points (e.g. F major "shift up on A, E string" вҖ”
   F6 is simply not there in 3rd position). Here they play the full first octave, then shift
   to 3rd position and climb the 2nd octave as far as the hand reaches (D6, or CвҷҜ6 for E
   major) before turning around.

It also surfaces two **Level 1** errors worth fixing in the source doc:

- **C major** listed as 2 octaves in 1st position вҖ” impossible (top C6 needs 3rd position).
  Make it 1 octave in 1st position, or move the 2-octave version to Level 2 with a shift.
- **D minor** listed as 2 octaves in 1st position вҖ” impossible (top D6 needs 3rd position).
  Same fix.

Only **C and D major, and C and D minor** genuinely require a 1stвҶ’3rd shift for their 2nd
octave. For all of them the clean shift is **on the E string, 1st finger guiding up to A5
(вҶ’ 3rd position)**, after which the top notes sit on the E string in 3rd position.
